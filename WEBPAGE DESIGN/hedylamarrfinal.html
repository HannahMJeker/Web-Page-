Biography

Hedy Lamarr was an Austrian actress, and inventor. She was born in 1913. Hedy was born as Hedwig Eva Maria Kiesler. Her parents were Emil kiesler, a bank director, and Gertrud Kiesler a pianist. As a child she was always interested in acting. She was discovered as a teenager by a producer named Max Reinhardt. Hedy started as a script girl before she really started acting.

She is often referred to as one of the most beautiful exotic actresses in Hollywood. She starred in many, many films that helped her gain her popularity and really blossom as an actress.

She became popular internationally in 1933. She acted in 18 fils in the early 1940's

During this time she also became interested in and involved in scientific research, next to George Antheil. Together they created a frequency hopping system.

This device helped radios "hop" from 1 frequency to another. This paved the way for the future of radios.

Accomplishments

Hedy has a star on the Hollywood walk of fame.

She received awards from The Electronic Frontier Foundation.

She has been inducted into the inventors hall of fame.
